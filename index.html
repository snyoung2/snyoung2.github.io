About the Young Lab

The Young Lab is now seeking both MS and PhD students for Fall 2019! Please email Dr. Young with a resume/CV and brief description of graduate school goals if interested.

Sierra Young is an Assistant Professor in the Biological and Agricultural Engineering Department at North Carolina State University. Broadly speaking, her research interests focus around the use of electronics, robotics, and automation for sensing and sense-making in natural and agricultural systems. She has expertise in deploying unmanned systems (including land, air, and surface vehicles) for hydrologic, environmental, and phenotyping applications.

The Young Lab aims to advance technical and human-centered design approaches for unmanned robotic and sensor systems to monitor agricultural and natural processes, while ensuring these systems are usable and adopted. Research in the Young Lab is done at the intersection of sensing technologies and robotics, data transformation and analytics, and decision support.
